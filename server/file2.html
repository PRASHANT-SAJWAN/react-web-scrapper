Are We Reaching the Tipping Point of Global Collapse?

The World America Made is Crumbling. Now What?

umair haque
Follow
Oct 14 · 8 min read

There’s a tiny question that keeps recurring to me these days. Have we reached the tipping point of global collapse? Are we past the point of no return when it comes to an immediate future of climate chaos, political upheaval, economic stagnation, and social implosion?

I have this sinking feeling that in recent months, we just might be getting pretty close. And you don’t want to play Russian roulette with the devil, my friends.

Let’s start with the global geopolitical situation. Extremism is still spreading rampant, unchecked, and swiftly. In America, 70% of Republican voters still believe the election was stolen. Even the world’s most powerful aren’t immune — in fact, they are some of the first to fall to the global tidal wave of authoritarianism.

In America, the Democrats have proven utterly incapable of restraining the authoritarians, who’ve launched a shadow campaign against democracy, The Dems have almost all the power it’s possible to have in American politics, and Biden is pushing an infrastructure bill that only gets America 2% of the way to becoming a functioning, modern society. Meanwhile, the GOP’s wave of totalitarianism at the state level is passing bills like the one in Texas, which lets bounty hunters catch women who want…bodily autonomy.

So America can’t be relied on to be a steward, guide, or navigator through these troubled times for the globe. In fact, precisely the opposite is true: America is what lies at the end of yesterday’s model of prosperity — capitalist-led financial growth, which left approximately 99% of society to live shorter, dumber, meaner, and poorer lives.

Then there’s Britain. There, a government so hard right that the like hasn’t been seen in rich countries since the 1930s has taken absolute power. What they wanted was very simple: to turn their backs on Europe, and become something like a mini-America. And, well, they’re getting what they asked for, and more — Because of Brexit, people in Britain are running short of food, gas, milk. And yet, in a turn of events that would make Orwell shudder, British media is calling this a “supply chain crisis” rather than what it is: a self-inflicted, Brexit-induced supply shock.

But there is a great deal of money in it for the right people — because Britain still has a few public goods, like the NHS and BBC, left for American-style capitalism to asset strip.

Now, I bring up the intertwined fates of the two historic Anglo countries for a reason. They are what happens when we get the future wrong. A vicious spiral sets in. People, getting poorer, elect extremists, usually right-wing extremists, if a society has a middle class that is imploding. But those right wing extremists make it impossible to solve any real problems, because they will not invest in anything, except, in America’s case, bigger guns. But you can’t solve the problems of a dying planet with a bigger gun.

Bang! The vicious spiral America and Britain are caught in erupts: poverty leads to right wing extremism leads to poverty leads to…and before you know it, your society has effectively committed suicide, like these two have. When this spiral sets in, the Big Four problems of the 21st century become impossible to solve. So let me continue.

Then there’s climate change. What about that? It seems to be hurtling past the point of no return, far faster than anyone, even the experts, expected. In the last few months, we’ve been told that we have just a decade left to hit the point of severe, irreversible global warming.

What are we doing? Not much, to be honest. The same old ineffectual treaties govern the same old world that is emitting the same old carbon, which is rising every year. We are on the path to climate catastrophe — and you are probably beginning to live it a little bit, whether through heatwave, flood, or downpour.

When are we going to get serious about not living on a dying planet? The grim answer is: not anytime soon.

You see, the vast majority of carbon emissions come from just a handful of corporations. Less than 100, in fact. You not eating meat or fish and so on has almost nothing to do with climate change — only assuaging your guilty ego.

Climate change is caused by capitalism. It isn’t caused by anything else. Certainly not you and me. Our consumption choices have almost no bearing on it whatsoever. The corporations will still burn the oil and chop down the trees and pollute the oceans, for whatever is profitable. “But that was our job!” they’ll say one day — the Nuremberg Defense. It comes too late when it must be used at all.

Therefore, to stop climate change, we’d need to reinvent and reimagine global capitalism. I mean that in a pragmatic and literal way. We’d need to start counting devastated forests and oceans and burning atmosphere in GDP, in “financial statements,” as well as the things countries call “capital accounts” and “current accounts.” Those things cannot just be made of fictional currencies anymore. Then, perhaps, we could punish those corporations for killing the planet — and deter others from doing so, too, having counted the damage done, and been able to do justice for it.

That way we would have a more realistic picture of our “economies” — instead of the bizarre illusion before us: the economy’s “growing,” while life, democracy, freedom, and prosperity are all dying. What the?

But there is absolutely no chance of reimagining global capitalism happening so far. Why not? Because, quite frankly, nobody has the imagination or wisdom or insight to do it. America and Britain’s thinkers don’t even understand such a thing is possible, let alone necessary. But they are the ones running the show. So who is going to change anything at all?

The global economy is capitalist because America made it so. The fact is that most of the world wanted to be a social democracy, from Chile to Argentina to Pakistan to India. America used armies to bomb country after country — or propaganda and coups to destabilize out their leadership — because they simply wanted to become social democracies. Not allowed, sorry — you can be capitalist, or you can be ruined, but you can’t be anything else.

That’s why incidentally, the only countries that are social democracies are rich ones — the ones America couldn’t bomb or wreck.

And then a decade or two later, it offered them “trade deals” (some of them, at least.) Arms enforced the rule of capitalism, the only deal on the table — capitalism, or destruction, take your pick.

See the point. America has absolutely no interest in anything but a capitalist global economy — and it will happily use everything from wars to propping up dictators to letting its megacorporations get away with anything to have it, down to giving them patents for vaccines that were created with public funds. The world was smart enough not to want it. But America had the muscle to make one — and now we are dealing with the consequences of a capitalist global economy, which are a dying planet, ravaged democracies, rising extremism, and mounting despair.

I want you to see the problem very, very clearly. The world has been trying to be a social democracy for decades now. America, though, wouldn’t let it happen, under any circumstances. Anything but a global economy that was capitalist was flatly impossible — unless you were foolish enough to want to be bombed, overthrown, or ruined. Lesson?

Today, a global economy that’s post-capitalist can’t emerge until America — or at least its establishment of mediocre, self-absorbed violent white men — finally fades to the background. Until America becomes a country that can’t impose its rules on the world, and back them with missiles, bombs, and coups. But that, ironically, while it’s happening, it isn’t happening fast enough, my friends. To what, exactly? To solve any of the other great global problems.

Climate change isn’t the only kind of toxic waste capitalism’s produced. There are also inequality, stagnation, and powerlessness.

And those three things are all mounting to crisis point, too. Inequality’s soaring in country after country — because capitalism concentrates capital in fewer and fewer hands. Hence, a tiny class of global ultra rich has emerged — while middle classes are either stuck, struggling, or outright imploding. (A class of global ultra-rich, who, by the way, could end Covid by vaccinating the world, without breaking a sweat — but won’t. But I digress.) Economy after economy is stagnant — because what they produce is hoarded by this tiny class, and the elites which now basically exist to defend and serve it. Hence, Chris Hayes probably makes multiple millions a year, for basically doing nothing a high school student can’t do — while the average teacher can’t make ends meet.

And because they are being overworked and underpaid by the system…who has time to change anything? When you’re struggling just to pay the bills…who has the energy left for democracy? When billionaires can buy all of Congress with a few checks…how can you fight it? Why bother fighting the system, when it’s already won to the point that it’s killing you? Hence, a crushing feeling of powerlessness is sweeping the globe.

By that, I don’t mean the very vocal and aggressive authoritarians — the Trumpists, the Brexiters, the Putinists. I mean that in country after country, there is now a silent majority. The vast majority of people don’t societies like the ones they are getting — whether in Britain, America, Europe, or even Asia. But those are the societies that the extremists are forcing upon them, because the majorities are silent. And the silent majority, too, is best seen as a product of implosive capitalism. When the consequences for speaking out are losing that job, that reputation, that livelihood, which is your only lifeline…when you can’t afford anything but silence financially, emotionally, socially, and culturally…you’ve become part of the silent majority.

We desperately need the following things as a world. A global economy that works for everyone, not just making the ultra rich richer. Functioning democracies, instead of polities captured by extremists. Attitudes and norms of courage and empathy and cooperation. Nations capable of treating every living being, from insects to trees to rivers, with dignity and respect. We don’t have any of those things — precisely because America’s contemptuous elites. would have rather had a world run by laser-guided drone missiles enforcing allegiance to capitalism than humanity, decency, respect, and justice.

But they are paying the price now — and so we all are, too.

One way to see all this is that American leadership has plunged the world into utter catastrophe. It led the world to the very same problems America’s now facing. Inequality, stagnation, extremism, pollution. Underinvestment, cruelty, ignorance, bigotry. A world can only be a model of its leader, after all. One day, history will ask, baffled — but why did the world allow itself to be led by a country that was essentially still a segregated apartheid state?

But all that is besides the larger point, perhaps.

Has the world reached a tipping point — beyond which lies an age of global chaos, catastrophe, and ruin? Does it feel like that point has been crossed — or is about to be — in recent months and days? Judge for yourself — and you tell me.

Umair
October 2021
